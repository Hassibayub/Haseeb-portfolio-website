## 1. Pre-Launch Checklist (do these FIRST)

Before you hit publish on ANY of the content below:

- [ ] Site is live at codewithhaseeb.com and every page loads without errors
- [ ] Mobile experience tested on real iPhone + Android
- [ ] Every CTA button (Book a Call, Send Message) works end-to-end
- [ ] Calendly booking tested from a different browser (not your own)
- [ ] Contact form tested — you received the test email via Resend
- [ ] All 8 case study pages have real cover images (not placeholders)
- [ ] GA4 events firing — open `analytics.google.com` → Reports → Realtime and confirm you see yourself
- [ ] Microsoft Clarity recording — confirm your session shows up
- [ ] Screenshot of the new site ready to attach to posts (1–2 good crops)
- [ ] LinkedIn profile updated with new site URL
- [ ] Upwork bio mentions new URL
- [ ] Email signature updated with new URL

If any of these are unchecked, **do not launch yet**. You don't get a second first impression.

## 2. LinkedIn Launch Post — 3 Variants

Pick one based on your comfort. My recommendation: **Variant A** (process + reflection, lowest risk, highest engagement for service businesses).

### Variant A — Process + reflection (RECOMMENDED)

Copy as-is, add your site screenshot, publish between 8–10am your time (higher reach).

---

```
Spent the last two weeks replacing my own website.

Old site: a portfolio template that made me look like a solo freelancer.
New site: a sales asset that reflects what we actually do.

The gap between the two was embarrassing.

A client flew me to talk about building a production AI system with
17,000+ active users. The same client then landed on a site with
"Download CV" as my primary button. Of course it didn't convert.

So we (my team of 5) rebuilt it over two weeks in NextJS. Same tech stack
we use for client work. Same quality bar. One accountable lead.

A few things I learned in the process:

→ Positioning comes before design. I tried three redesigns on the old
  positioning and each one was worse than the last.

→ Case studies need their own URLs. Not a scrolling list, not a popup.
  Pages you can DM, link in proposals, rank in search.

→ Your best proof point is the one you're most scared to claim. For me
  that was naming YC-backed Tula ($1.2M raised), not softening the claim
  to "funded startup."

→ Pick an accent color that matches positioning. I went with deep purple
  because AI brands (Anthropic, Linear, etc.) own that space, and I wanted
  to signal "production AI" not "fintech SaaS."

→ Show the team. Freelancer profiles convert worse than agency profiles
  because buyers fear flakiness. A real 5-person senior team on display
  changes how pricing gets evaluated.

Live at codewithhaseeb.com. Feedback welcome.

Special thanks to [team member names if you want to shout them out]
for building alongside me.

What's the worst mistake you made on your own marketing site?
```

**Why this works:**
- Opens with vulnerability ("embarrassing") — triggers engagement psychology
- Concrete specifics, not platitudes
- Lessons-learned format invites comments with others' experiences
- Closing question primes engagement
- Drops the URL once, naturally

## 3. Personal Email to Past 10 Clients

Reach out individually to your top past clients. Not a blast. Not CC'd. One at a time, personalized.

### Subject line options (pick one)

- `Quick update — new site, same team`
- `[First name], we finally rebuilt the site`
- `Something I wanted you to see first`
- `New codewithhaseeb.com — and a thanks`

Best: the third one (curiosity + warmth). Test both.

### Template

```
Hey [First name],

Quick one — we just relaunched codewithhaseeb.com with a proper case
study section, and I wanted to send it to you before posting publicly.

Your project with us ([specific project name / outcome]) is one of the
wins we're now showcasing. Here's the page: codewithhaseeb.com/work/[slug]

Two quick asks:
1. Take a look and let me know if anything is off, and especially if
   there's something you'd like me to adjust, remove, or strengthen.

2. If you know anyone building AI products who'd benefit from a team
   like ours, I'd love an intro. Taking on 1 new project this month.

Either way — thank you for trusting us on [specific thing about the
engagement, e.g., "the ridiculous 6-week deadline on the voice agent"
or "trusting a team from Pakistan with your production stack"]. The
case study wouldn't exist without what you built alongside us.

— Haseeb

codewithhaseeb.com
```

### Personalization rules

- Replace `[First name]` with actual name (obviously)
- Replace `[specific project name]` with their project
- Link to their specific case study page, not the home page
- Add ONE specific detail in the thank-you paragraph ("your trust on the 6-week deadline") — this is what makes it feel real, not templated
- If the client is mentioned publicly on the case study, link directly
- If the project is under NDA or the client prefers anonymity, drop the case study link and just invite feedback on the site overall

### Who to send this to

Your top 10 most relationship-dense past clients. Specifically:
- Every client with a 5-star Upwork review
- Every client you'd take a second project from
- Every client who's currently at a company that might have a network of AI-buying peers

Don't blast everyone. 10 personalized emails > 100 mass emails.

### Expected response rate

- Read rate: 70–90% (personal emails get read)
- Reply rate: 30–50% with some positive feedback or small edit
- Referral rate: 10–20% (1–2 out of 10 will think of someone)
- Conversion: if even one referral turns into a client, this email recovered 100× the time you spent writing it

## 8. Community Posts (Hacker News, Reddit, Indie Hackers)

Risky. Community posts work only if you avoid sounding promotional. Otherwise skip.

### Hacker News "Show HN"

**Title:**
```
Show HN: My agency's new site — feedback welcome
```

**Body (keep it short):**
```
Long-time HN reader. Just relaunched codewithhaseeb.com — we're a 5-person
team shipping production AI (Aphra.me, Tula, Capwell, etc.).

Built on NextJS + Tailwind + shadcn. Would appreciate feedback from this
crowd, especially on:

- Is the positioning ("AI that runs in production, not demos") clear?
- Case study pages readable enough?
- Anything technically broken (mobile, SEO, performance)?

Thanks.
```

**Timing:** submit between 7–9am EST Tuesday–Thursday.

**Expected:** modest traction. HN is harsh on anything that looks like marketing. But the feedback is honest.

### Reddit — r/EntrepreneurRideAlong or r/SaaS

Only post if you have real community presence there. Pure cold posting gets downvoted.

### Indie Hackers

Write a longer "Show IH" post — they reward thoughtful depth:

```
Title: Rebuilt my portfolio site → now a proper agency sales asset

Body: [Copy the LinkedIn Variant A post above, with small edits to match
the IH voice — slightly more personal]
```

## 9. Follow-Up Content (Week 2 and Beyond)

Don't stop at launch day. The content that moves real business is the follow-up.

### Week 2 — Deep-dive case study post

Pick your most impressive single case study — I'd pick **KCNL** because "$100K/mo → $1.5K/mo" is your most shareable stat — and turn it into a dedicated LinkedIn post or blog article.

```
Title: How we cut a client's LLM bill from $100,000/month to $1,500/month

Body: [detailed story, same structure as the case study page but
narrated first-person from your perspective, with specific tactical
lessons other AI builders can apply]

Ends with: "Full case study: codewithhaseeb.com/work/kcnl"
```

### Week 3 — "Mistakes I made" post

People share failures more than wins. Write about a specific failure you've seen with AI projects (bad scoping, wrong model choice, cost runaway) and what you learned.

### Week 4 — Hiring announcement (if you're hiring)

If you're hiring engineer #6 for the team, announce it on the new site's blog + LinkedIn. Incoming engineers who find you via the new site = a recruiting flywheel.

### Monthly — Case study drip

Pick one case study per month and do a deep-dive post about it. In 6 months you'll have 6 in-depth posts circulating, each driving traffic back to the site.

## 10. Timing & Sequencing Guide

### Launch day (Day 0)

Morning (your time):
1. Final pre-launch checklist (Section 1)
2. Point DNS / flip site live
3. Test everything one more time from incognito browser

Mid-morning:
4. Post **LinkedIn Variant A** (9–10am local)
5. Post **Twitter thread** if you use Twitter
6. Update Upwork bio banner

Afternoon:
7. Send the first 3 personal emails (Section 3) to your highest-trust past clients
8. Respond to LinkedIn comments as they come in

Evening:
9. Send 3–4 more personal emails (stagger through the week, not all in one day)
10. Review GA4 realtime — see if launch traffic is actually landing correctly

### Day 1–3

- Send remaining 3–4 personal client emails (pacing)
- Monitor GA4 events firing correctly
- Monitor Microsoft Clarity — watch 3–5 session replays to see how real visitors move through the new site
- Fix any small bugs that emerge from real usage
- Reply to every LinkedIn comment

### Day 4–7

- Update Contra profile + post new work item
- Post in Indie Hackers (if relevant)
- Post in Hacker News (if confident enough for HN feedback)
- Start Week 2 content planning

### Week 2 onwards

- Start the deep-dive case study posts (Section 9)
- LinkedIn cadence: 2 posts per week minimum
- Monitor conversion events — are people actually booking calls?
- Iterate: look at Clarity heatmaps, find drop-off points, adjust

### First 30 days — success metrics to track

| Metric | Target (30 days) | Where to check |
|---|---|---|
| New website users | 500+ (10× current) | GA4 |
| Book-a-call clicks | 10+ | GA4 events |
| Contact form submissions | 5+ | Resend inbox |
| Calendly bookings | 3+ | Calendly dashboard |
| LinkedIn launch post views | 10K+ | LinkedIn analytics |
| Case study page views | 100+ across all 8 | GA4 by page path |
| Inbound referrals from clients | 1+ | Email / DM |

If you hit these, the launch worked. If you miss them significantly, we diagnose and adjust (could be site issue, could be content issue, could be channel issue).

## Don't Forget

The launch is the **start**, not the end. The site is a compounding asset that gets better with:
- More case studies added as new projects ship
- Blog posts written and indexed by Google
- Backlinks built through LinkedIn posts, client testimonials, podcast appearances
- Testimonial quotes added as new reviews come in on Upwork / LinkedIn

6 months of consistent updates + content will 10× the impact of launch day. Plan for the long game.
